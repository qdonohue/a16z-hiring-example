# Growth Engineer Fellowship - Evaluation Criteria

## Scoring Dimensions (1-5 scale each)

### 1. Builder DNA (Weight: 30%)
- **5 - Exceptional:** Ships constantly, can walk through technical architecture of systems they've built, shows evidence of rapid iteration and experimentation. Has built things that actually moved metrics.
- **4 - Strong:** Clearly builds and ships regularly, can discuss implementation details, has tangible results.
- **3 - Adequate:** Has built things but may rely heavily on no-code or is more of a strategist who dabbles in building.
- **2 - Developing:** Mostly strategic/managerial, doesn't get hands dirty with implementation.
- **1 - Insufficient:** No evidence of building or shipping.

### 2. AI-Native Thinking (Weight: 25%)
- **5 - Exceptional:** AI is embedded in their core workflow. Has production AI systems running. Thinks about AI as infrastructure, not a feature. Can articulate non-obvious applications.
- **4 - Strong:** Actively using AI tools in growth work, has at least one meaningful AI-powered system, understands capabilities and limitations.
- **3 - Adequate:** Uses ChatGPT/Copilot but hasn't built AI-native growth systems. Interested but not yet operational.
- **2 - Developing:** Aware of AI but hasn't meaningfully integrated it into their work.
- **1 - Insufficient:** Not using AI in any meaningful way.

### 3. Growth Intuition (Weight: 20%)
- **5 - Exceptional:** Instantly identifies the binding constraint in a growth problem. Thinks in systems. Can reason about funnels, activation, retention, and monetization with specificity. Strong pattern recognition across business types.
- **4 - Strong:** Good growth instincts, can diagnose problems methodically, understands key metrics.
- **3 - Adequate:** Knows growth fundamentals but analysis stays surface-level.
- **2 - Developing:** Understands growth conceptually but can't apply it to novel situations.
- **1 - Insufficient:** No meaningful growth intuition.

### 4. Curiosity & Learning Velocity (Weight: 15%)
- **5 - Exceptional:** Voracious learner, explores adjacent domains, can connect dots between unrelated fields. Obviously stays on the bleeding edge.
- **4 - Strong:** Clearly learning continuously, can reference recent experiments or discoveries.
- **3 - Adequate:** Keeps up with the field but doesn't go deep or explore widely.
- **2 - Developing:** Relies on established knowledge, not actively expanding.
- **1 - Insufficient:** Stagnant, no evidence of ongoing learning.

### 5. Community Energy (Weight: 10%)
- **5 - Exceptional:** Would clearly elevate the cohort. Shares openly, gives thoughtful feedback, asks great questions. Has infectious energy about the work.
- **4 - Strong:** Collaborative, would contribute meaningfully to group sessions.
- **3 - Adequate:** Would participate but might not proactively contribute.
- **2 - Developing:** Seems more interested in taking than giving.
- **1 - Insufficient:** Not a fit for a collaborative cohort.

## Segmentation Thresholds

### Top 5% — Auto-Schedule with Partner (Weighted Score >= 4.5)
- Exceptional across the board — this person is clearly the real deal
- At least two "Exceptional" (5) ratings, no dimension below 4
- You'd bet money they'll be one of the best fellows in the cohort
- **Action:** Automatically schedule a 30-minute call on a GP's calendar. No friction, no extra steps. These people shouldn't wait.

### 70th–95th Percentile — Follow-Up Interview (Weighted Score 3.5 - 4.4)
- Strong signal but want to go deeper on one or two dimensions
- Promising builder with clear strengths, but some gaps worth probing
- **Action:** Send a follow-up email inviting them to a deeper screening conversation with an AI interviewer that digs into their specific gap areas

### Bottom 70% — Soft Pass (Weighted Score < 3.5)
- Not ready for this cohort, or not the right fit
- May have potential but significant gaps in builder DNA or AI-native thinking
- **Action:** Send a warm rejection email — thank them, acknowledge their interest, and let them know we'll keep them in the network for future opportunities. Be specific about what would strengthen a future application.

## Red Flags (Automatic concerns)
- Can't describe anything they've personally built (only managed/directed)
- AI usage is limited to "I use ChatGPT for writing" with no systems thinking
- No specific metrics or outcomes from their work
- Dismissive of other people's approaches or tools
- Zero questions about the fellowship or cohort (low curiosity signal)

## Green Flags (Strong positive signals)
- Shows you something they built during the conversation (links, demos, screenshots)
- Unprompted deep-dive into HOW something works technically
- Mentions failed experiments with clear learnings
- Asks thoughtful questions about the fellowship format or other fellows
- Connects ideas across domains (e.g., applies game design to onboarding)
- Obviously can't stop tinkering — talks about side projects, experiments, rabbit holes
