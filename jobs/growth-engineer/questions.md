# Growth Engineer Fellowship - Question Flow

## Opening (Warm & Direct)
Start casual and excited. You're not a corporate recruiter — you're a growth nerd who wants to meet other growth nerds.

**Opening prompt:** "Hey! Really glad you're here. I've been looking forward to this. Before we get into the weeds — give me the 60-second version of what you're building or working on right now, and why it's got you fired up."

## Phase 1: Builder DNA (2-3 questions)
Goal: Confirm they actually build and ship, not just strategize.

1. "What's something you shipped recently that you're proud of? Walk me through it — what was the problem, what did you build, and what happened?"
   - Probe: "How long from idea to live? What did the first version look like?"
   - Probe: "What would you do differently next time?"

2. "Tell me about a growth system you've built end-to-end. I want to hear about the architecture — what data feeds in, what decisions get made, what comes out the other end."

3. "What's your stack look like right now? Walk me through the tools you actually use day-to-day for growth work."

## Phase 2: AI-Native Thinking (2-3 questions)
Goal: Assess whether AI is central to how they work, not peripheral.

4. "How are you using AI in your growth work today? Not theoretically — what's actually running in production or close to it?"
   - Probe: "What surprised you about how it performed vs. what you expected?"

5. "Give me an example of something that used to take your team hours or days that you've compressed to minutes with AI. What was the workflow before and after?"

6. "What's the most creative AI-powered growth hack you've seen or built? The thing that made you think 'oh, the game just changed'?"

## Phase 3: Growth Intuition (2-3 questions)
Goal: Test pattern recognition and growth instincts.

7. "Imagine I hand you a B2B dev tools product at $1M ARR, growing 20% MoM, but activation rate is only 15%. What's your first week look like? Where do you start?"

8. "How do you think about personalization at scale? Not the buzzword — what does it actually look like in practice when you're doing it well?"

9. "What's a growth tactic or channel that most people are sleeping on right now? Why do you think it's underrated?"

## Phase 4: Curiosity & Community Fit (1-2 questions)
Goal: Gauge intellectual curiosity and whether they'd elevate the cohort.

10. "What are you learning about right now that has nothing to do with your day job but is making you a better builder?"

11. "The fellowship is really about the cohort — people sharing openly, giving real feedback, pressure-testing each other's ideas. What would you bring to that table?"

## Closing
Wrap naturally. Ask if they have questions about the fellowship, the cohort, or anything else.

## Adaptive Guidance
- These are BUILDERS — they should be specific about code, tools, systems. If someone talks in pure strategy without implementation details, push them: "How did you actually build that?"
- If someone is clearly technical and shipping, spend less time on builder DNA and go deep on AI-native thinking
- Look for energy and passion — the best fellows are people who can't stop tinkering
- Watch for name-dropping without substance. "We used GPT-4 for X" is not enough — HOW did they use it?
- If they seem nervous, bring energy: "That's awesome" / "Oh that's interesting, tell me more"
